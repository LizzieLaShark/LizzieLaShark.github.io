So far I've been enjoying the book, though I find the author does a lot of qualifying before he goes the discussion. It made me think that he thinks his audience is a bunch of people who don't actually want to be reading his book! Maybe he's right...
Another reflection has been that it's quite confronting for me to read about mindfulness and meditation in the context of making money and progressing your career. Sentences like the following:

"...in other words, what if contemplative practices can be made beneficial both to people's careers and to business bottom lines?"

"I hope your journey will be fun and profitable"

It's interesting because I have always thought of mindfulness as one of many practices and tools that could help (for want of a better word) revolutionise the world and facilitate a shift towards a more thriving planet. To me, profit and business bottom lines are not compatible with this, so it's a little jarring to hear someone talk about such things in the context of mindfulness and meditation. Despite this, I can empathise with his attempt to de-hippify the practise, and I appreciate this. Tan is coming from a good place, his wrting is easy and pleasant to read, I'm looking forward to reading more from him.

My two minutes of mindfulness were nice and relaxing. I really enjoy meditation, but even after years of trying I still find it very difficult to keep up a regular practice. I appreciate how bite-sized Tan makes it seem. I'm finding that to be quite a theme over the last month or so, breaking stuff into bite-sized bits. So far it's really helping in other areas of my life so I think that this approach to mindfulness will be really valuable.
